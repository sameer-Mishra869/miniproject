Frequently Asked Questions

Everything you need to know about Milano Cortina 2026

General Information

Where and when will the 2026 Olympic Winter Games take place?

+

The 2026 Olympic Winter Games will take place from February 6-22, 2026 in Milano Cortina, Italy. Events will be held across multiple venues in Milan, Cortina d'Ampezzo, Bormio, Livigno, Antholz-Anterselva, Predazzo, Tesero, and Verona.

How many countries will participate in the Winter Olympics 2026?

+

Approximately 95 countries are expected to participate in the Milano Cortina 2026 Winter Olympics, with over 2,900 athletes competing across 15 sports and 116 events.

What is the mascot of the Winter Olympics 2026?

+

The mascots of Milano Cortina 2026 are Tina and Milo - two adorable stoats representing the spirit of the Italian Alps. Tina represents the snow, while Milo represents the ice. Together, they embody energy, passion, and the love for winter sports.

What is the slogan of Milano Cortina 2026?

+

The official slogan is "Together for Tomorrow", reflecting the Games' commitment to sustainability and unity through sport.

Tickets & Attendance

How to get tickets for the Winter Olympics 2026?

+

Tickets for Milano Cortina 2026 can be purchased through:

  • The official Olympics website (olympics.com)
  • Official Milano Cortina 2026 ticketing portal
  • Authorized National Olympic Committee resellers

Various ticket packages are available for different events and ceremonies, including single event tickets and multi-event packages.

What is the price range for Olympic tickets?

+

Ticket prices vary depending on the event and seating category:

  • Opening/Closing Ceremony: €200 - €1,500
  • Popular events (Figure Skating, Ice Hockey): €50 - €300
  • Alpine Skiing: €30 - €150
  • Other events: €20 - €100

Can I attend the Opening Ceremony?

+

Yes! The Opening Ceremony will be held at San Siro Stadium in Milan on February 6, 2026. Tickets are available through official channels. The Closing Ceremony will take place at the historic Arena di Verona on February 22, 2026.

Sports & Events

What sports are in the Olympic Winter Games Milano Cortina 2026?

+

The Games will feature 15 sports and 116 events:

  • Alpine Skiing (11 events)
  • Biathlon (11 events)
  • Bobsled (4 events)
  • Cross-Country Skiing (12 events)
  • Curling (3 events)
  • Figure Skating (5 events)
  • Freestyle Skiing (13 events)
  • Ice Hockey (2 events)
  • Luge (4 events)
  • Nordic Combined (3 events)
  • Short Track Speed Skating (9 events)
  • Skeleton (2 events)
  • Ski Jumping (5 events)
  • Snowboard (11 events)
  • Speed Skating (14 events)

What new sports will be at the Olympic Winter Games Milano Cortina 2026?

+

Milano Cortina 2026 will feature Ski Mountaineering as a new Olympic sport. This exciting addition combines climbing and skiing, with athletes racing up and down mountain terrain.

How to qualify for the Winter Olympics 2026?

+

Athletes qualify through:

  • World Championship results
  • World Cup standings
  • Continental championships
  • Qualification events specific to each sport

Each sport has its own qualification criteria set by the International Federation. Athletes must also meet their National Olympic Committee's selection standards.

Venues & Locations

How many venues will there be at the Olympic Winter Games Milano Cortina 2026?

+

Milano Cortina 2026 will use approximately 14 competition venues across 8 venue clusters:

  • Milano - Ice events, Opening Ceremony
  • Cortina d'Ampezzo - Alpine Skiing, Sliding sports, Curling
  • Bormio - Alpine Skiing (speed events)
  • Livigno - Freestyle Skiing, Snowboard
  • Antholz-Anterselva - Biathlon
  • Predazzo - Ski Jumping, Nordic Combined
  • Tesero - Cross-Country Skiing
  • Verona - Closing Ceremony

Where will the Opening and Closing Ceremonies be held?

+

The Opening Ceremony will take place at San Siro Stadium in Milan on February 6, 2026. San Siro is one of the most iconic football stadiums in the world.

The Closing Ceremony will be held at the historic Arena di Verona on February 22, 2026. This ancient Roman amphitheatre, built in the 1st century, will provide a spectacular backdrop for the closing celebration.

What time zone are the events in?

+

All events will be held in Central European Time (CET), which is UTC+1. During the Games period, Italy will be in CET (not daylight saving time).
